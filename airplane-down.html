Airplane Down is an outpost made for Far Cry 5. The objective is to eliminate all enemies in outpost on an American farm where airplane crashed.

OVERVIEW

NEW EXPERIENCE

When I opened the editor I already liked it. It was so intuitive that without any tutorials I started exploring its possibilities. From the very beginning I wanted to make something in Far Cry 5 vibes because I liked level design in this game.

​

In the beginning I tried to get the feeling of the map and make boundaries of the gameplay area as natural as possible. Also I paid attention to a farm to be made so that it can work in real life. I based on reference photos and in game locations.

​

I added crashed airplane to make my map stand out from the rest and to add some character, uniqueness and new possibilities to the map.

When I was happy with the feel of the map I focused more on player experience and first impressions. I have designed it to accommodate various playstyles and at the same time challenge experienced players. I tried to give a player a clear way to outpost and vantage point visible when a player is spawned.

I had in mind lessons from the last project and I build a world to be easy to read. The landmark is a big mountain with a waterfall that creates a river which goes through the map to the other end of it. There are also a windmill and silos to build the credibility of the locations which also serve as landmarks.

AI AND CHALLENGE

I didn't have experience with controlling AI before so I had to learn it. In FC5 AI is quite basic so it was fairly easy to set up enemies and start thinking about player approaches.

​

The player can access the outpost from every angle because of the short fence. To make it more challenging I added AI patrolling boundaries of the outpost. So for the player to get inside unnoticed, must find a gap in security. The outpost is made so that the closer to the center there are more opponents and more movement. If a failed alarm goes off and reinforcements arrive (13 enemies in the outpost, 8 enemies coming after an alarm from 2 points spawn). To forgive player mistakes I deployed ammo, health and armor around the outpost.

Then I made what I'm calling 'Rambo path'. Aggressive playstyle for players wanting action. If the player decided to attack the outpost from the front without any preparation it will be the hardest challenge for him. I rewarded this approach with explosive barrels close to opponents.

If the player decides to go to the left side he will encounter a sneaky path in the river leading to a crashed airplane with a perfect view from the wing for sniper class. On the other side of the water, I made walls from rocks to visually tell where is the end of the play area.

Lastly right side of the map. This path is good for sneaky approach and disabling alarm in a good moment after eliminating only one enemy.

POLISHING AND PUBLISHING

At the end of the process, I hit the objects limit in the center of the outpost so I had to balance well between AI, gameplay props and decorations. I did playtest map by myself a lot of times and it was a fun experience. I learned a lot about singleplayer maps, mapping in general and AI.

​

I'm looking forward to the future and I can't wait to put my hands on the next projects!
